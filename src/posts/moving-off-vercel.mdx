this isn't a food blog recipe with a life story before the ingredients. here's what i make almost every tuesday when i don't feel like thinking about dinner.

## what you need

- leftover rice (day-old, cold from the fridge — this matters)
- 1 egg
- sesame oil
- soy sauce
- whatever vegetables are about to go bad
- chili crisp if you have it

## the move

get a pan hot. like, really hot. add a little sesame oil and toss in the cold rice. don't touch it for 2 minutes — you want the bottom to get crispy. that's the whole secret.

while the rice is doing its thing, fry an egg in a separate pan. runny yolk. non-negotiable.

> the best meals i've ever had weren't complicated. they were just *timed right*.

chop whatever vegetables you found — half a bell pepper, some green onions, that sad-looking broccoli. toss them in with the rice for the last minute. hit it with soy sauce. slide the egg on top. chili crisp if you're feeling it.

## why tuesday

monday you're still recovering from the weekend. wednesday you have energy to cook something real. tuesday is the dead zone — you need a meal that asks nothing of you and gives you everything back.

this is that meal.
